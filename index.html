Have you heard about Gabrovo?

Gabrovo is a lovely town in central northern Bulgaria known for its unique nature, traditions and culture. Home of the biggest carnival in Bulgaria, Gabrovo is also known as an international capital of humour and satire attracting many tourists each year. Stretching over 25 km along the Yantra River makes Gabrovo the longest town in the country. The geographic center of Bulgaria - Uzana is located near the town. Great weather and great people make the perfect combination for a holiday for those who love the mountains with an opportunity to try the famous ski slopes in the winter and climb some of the highest peaks in Bulgaria.

Name

According to the most widespread legend, Gabrovo was founded by a blacksmith called Racho, close to whose fireplace a hornbeam rose, so the settlement acquired its name, from the Bulgarian word gabar ("hornbeam") + the Bulgarian suffix -ovo.

Geography

The town of Gabrovo is situated in the northern branches of the Central Balkan Mountains with altitude of 392 m. The average annual temperature is 10° C. Cold winter(the snow cover lasts about 110 days), summer-relatively warm reaching temperatures up to 40 ° C.


See a bigger map

The proximity of Gabrovo to the Shipka pass is defined as an important transport node. The city is 220 km away. from Sofia(the capital). The townis a part of one of the most important road links crossing Bulgaria in the North-South direction, which is part of the Trans-European Transport Corridor № 9. The town is also a regional center of Gabrovo region. Gabrovo municipality consists of 10 mayoralties and 18 mayoralties. Situated on an area of ​​1871.7 ha, Gabrovo is also a strategic transport crossroad on the E85 main road.
